was born in Chapel Hill, North Carolina in 1995. He received Bachelor of Fine Arts degrees in Sculpture and Printmaking from Savannah College of Art and Design in Atlanta, Georgia. Using drawing, video, printed matter, and publications his work explores the material realities of visual communication, which extends into a critical investigation of aesthetic production as a site of labor.

His most recent solo exhibition was held at Whitespec in Atlanta, Georgia (2019). Other recent exhibitions include a solo exhibition at The B-Complex, Atlanta (2019), as well as group exhibitions at Hathaway Gallery, Atlanta (2018); Besharat Museum Gallery, Atlanta (2018); and Gutstein Gallery, Savannah (2017). Scholtz lives and works in New York.
